# Simply Perfect Pancakes — normalized recipe fragment

The recipe describes a batch of simple pancakes made from eggs, milk, fat, flour, salt, baking powder, and sweetener. Blueberries are optional.

The ingredients are:

- 2 large eggs.
- Milk: normally 1 1/4 cups, approximately 283 g. Use 1 cup of milk in hot and humid conditions or if the batter will rest longer than 15 minutes. Use up to 1 1/4 cups of milk in cold and dry conditions.
- Melted butter or vegetable oil: 3 tablespoons, approximately 43 g.
- All-purpose flour: 1 1/2 cups, approximately 180 g.
- Table salt: 3/4 teaspoon.
- Baking powder: 2 teaspoons.
- Sweetener: either 2 tablespoons granulated sugar, approximately 25 g, or 1/4 cup malted milk powder, approximately 35 g.
- Fresh or frozen blueberries: optional.

The preparation process is ordered.

Step 1: Beat the eggs and milk together until the mixture is light and foamy. This takes about 3 minutes at high speed with a stand mixer or hand mixer. Stir in the melted butter or vegetable oil.

Step 2: In a separate bowl, whisk the dry ingredients together so that the salt, baking powder, and sweetener are evenly distributed through the flour.

Step 3: Combine the dry ingredient mixture with the egg-and-milk mixture. Mix gently and quickly. Let the batter rest for at least 15 minutes while the cooking surface heats. The batter thickens slightly while resting.

Step 4: Heat a heavy frying pan over medium heat, or set an electric griddle to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Lightly grease the frying pan or griddle. The cooking surface is ready when a drop of water skitters across it and evaporates immediately.

Step 5: For each pancake, drop 1/4 cup of batter onto the lightly greased cooking surface. If making blueberry pancakes, sprinkle fresh blueberries or thawed frozen blueberries over the batter after it is portioned. Do not mix frozen blueberries into the batter before scooping if a blue and streaky batter should be avoided.

Step 6: Cook the first side until bubbles form and begin to break, about 2 minutes. Turn each pancake once. Cook the second side until brown, about 1 1/2 to 2 minutes. Serve immediately.

The recipe can be adapted into waffles by replacing 2 tablespoons of the milk with 2 tablespoons of melted butter or vegetable oil and cooking the batter in a waffle iron. A whole-grain variation replaces 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour with 1/2 cup of ground oats.
