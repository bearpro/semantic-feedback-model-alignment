# Synthetic industrial standard fragment — hexagon-head bolt

This fragment specifies a hexagon-head bolt for general industrial assembly. It is a synthetic standard-like fragment prepared for an experiment and is not an official standard.

The product is a hexagon-head bolt used for fastening steel parts in indoor industrial equipment. The bolt consists of a hexagonal head, a cylindrical shank, an external metric thread, a bearing surface under the head, an optional washer, an optional surface coating, and a head marking. The bolt is intended to be used with a matching internal metric thread or a compatible nut.

The nominal size of the bolt is defined by nominal thread diameter, thread pitch, and bolt length. Bolt length is measured from the bearing surface under the head to the end of the threaded tip. The allowed nominal thread diameters in this fragment are M6, M8, M10, and M12. The standard coarse pitch for M6 is 1.0 mm. The standard coarse pitch for M8 is 1.25 mm. The standard coarse pitch for M10 is 1.5 mm. The standard coarse pitch for M12 is 1.75 mm. Allowed nominal bolt lengths are from 20 mm to 120 mm inclusive.

The nominal head dimensions are defined by size. For M6, the width across flats is 10 mm and the head height is 4.0 mm. For M8, the width across flats is 13 mm and the head height is 5.3 mm. For M10, the width across flats is 16 mm and the head height is 6.4 mm. For M12, the width across flats is 18 mm and the head height is 7.5 mm.

The thread shall be complete enough to engage the matching nut or internal thread. The incomplete thread at the end of the bolt shall not exceed two thread pitches. A bolt shall not have burrs, cracks, sharp projections, or visible corrosion. The bearing surface under the head shall be flat enough to seat against a washer or against the fastened part. The head bearing surface shall be approximately perpendicular to the bolt axis.

The bolt material shall be either carbon steel property class 8.8 or stainless steel property class A2-70. Carbon steel bolts shall be marked with the property class on the top of the head. Stainless steel bolts shall be marked with the stainless property class when the head size permits. The manufacturer mark shall be applied to the head when the head size permits. Marking shall remain legible after normal handling and packaging.

A surface coating may be zinc plating, black oxide, or no coating. If the bolt is coated, the coating shall not prevent the thread from engaging a compatible nut by hand for at least three full turns. Zinc-plated bolts shall not show red corrosion visible to the naked eye before delivery. Black-oxide bolts shall be supplied with a corrosion-inhibiting oil film unless the purchase order requires a dry finish.

A bolt may be supplied with a flat washer. If supplied, the washer shall have a hole diameter compatible with the nominal bolt diameter and shall sit under the bolt head. The washer shall not be permanently attached to the bolt unless the product is explicitly identified as a washer-assembled bolt.

The installed joint shall provide at least one nominal bolt diameter of thread engagement. For example, an M8 bolt shall have at least 8 mm of engaged thread after installation. The bolt shall not be used for lifting equipment, pressure vessels, or fatigue-critical joints unless the design document explicitly permits that use. A bolt may be reused only if the head, shank, thread, and bearing surface show no visible damage.

Each package shall identify the nominal diameter, pitch, length, material property class, coating, quantity, batch identifier, and manufacturer. The batch identifier shall allow traceability to the production lot and inspection record.

Inspection shall be performed on each batch. The sample size shall be at least 5 bolts or 1 percent of the batch quantity, whichever is greater. If one sample bolt fails a dimensional or visual requirement, a second sample with twice the original sample size shall be inspected. If any bolt in the second sample fails the same requirement, the batch shall be rejected or sorted before release.
